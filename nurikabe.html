Rules

  • Each numbered cell is an island cell, the number in it is the number of cells in that island
  • Each island must contain exactly one numbered cell
  • There must be only one sea, which is not allowed to contain "pools", which means 2x2 areas of black cells
  • Cells are initially of unknown color, but can only be black or white. Two same-color cells are considered "connected" if they are adjacent vertically or horizontally, but not diagonally. Connected white cells form "islands", while connected black cells form the "sea". Two islands may only touch at corners. And any cells marked with '1' at the outset are complete islands unto themselves, and can be isolated with black at the beginning.
